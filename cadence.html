The spacing law — proven by every discount we ran

Each bar is a base-game discount event (launch months Jun–Jul 2024 excluded — see note). The pattern is blunt: run two sales too close and the second one flops.

✗ 1 month apart → flops

A sale right after another barely sells. The pile was just drained — nothing left to convert.

✓ 2–3 months apart → works

Give the pile time to refill and the sale fires properly. This is the safe cadence.

The takeaway: 2 months is the floor, never tighter. Every quick back-to-back sale flopped (880, then ~11k). Every well-spaced one worked or boomed (18k, 50k, 90k, 100k). "Every 2 months" sits right on the edge — it works, but you cannot compress it further.

Note: Jun & Jul 2024 are excluded — that was the game's launch surge (fresh demand, full pile), so even a back-to-back sale rode the launch wave. A one-time exception, not the steady-state pattern.

Two things we tell BNE straight

We trade global reach for control

Some past spikes rode the global Steam sale's discovery and front-page featuring. A China-only window won't get that push — it leans entirely on the China channel's own reach. Whether the channel replaces that traffic is the real open question. We flag it, we don't hide it.

The year becomes fully promo-paced

Several windows a year risks training buyers to only ever wait for a discount. But the data shows they already behave that way — the base game sells nothing at full price regardless. So there's no full-price business to lose. The risk is largely already priced in.

The forecast for Bandai — net-new redemptions

Supply is unlimited (Bandai mints codes on demand — no inventory cap). The driver is demand per window. These numbers are deliberately conservative: anchored to recent 35%-off events, then haircut twice — once for losing global Steam traffic (China-only), and again because Elden Ring is a 5-year-old game and discount volumes decay every year (Dec 2024 = 90k → Dec 2025 = 29k, same sale, one year later). Base sells ~0 at full price, so nearly all of this is net-new revenue Bandai would otherwise never get.

The pitch to Bandai, in one line: across four China-only windows, ~60,000 net-new redemptions a year (range 36k–97k) from a 5-year-old title that currently earns almost nothing between sales — and it's nearly all incremental, because the base game sells ~0 at full price. Bandai keeps full global pricing everywhere else.

Why conservative is right here: ER is ~5 years old (launched Feb 2022). The pool of un-bought Chinese buyers shrinks every sale cycle, and the data shows it — the same Winter discount pulled 90k in 2024 and 29k in 2025. We anchored to the recent, declined events and haircut again for FY27 aging + China-only. Better to under-promise and beat it. * Feb Nightreign is an EPICSOFT estimate (not in our royalty data) — Bandai validates that one.

The strategic flip: a 5-year-old game earning ~0 between sales is the argument FOR the cadence, not against. There's no healthy full-price business to cannibalize — targeted China discounts are how you keep monetizing a mature title.
