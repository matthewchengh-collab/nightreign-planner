The every-2-month China-exclusive sales cadence

The idea: pull Elden Ring out of the global Steam sales and run China-only discount windows on our own ~2-month cadence — dates we control, walled off from global pricing and the Switch 2 freeze. October is window #1, not a one-off.

The spacing law — proven by every discount we ran

Each bar is a base-game discount event (launch months Jun–Jul 2024 excluded — see note). The pattern is blunt: run two sales too close and the second one flops.

✗ 1 month apart → flops

A sale right after another barely sells. The pile was just drained — nothing left to convert.

✓ 2–3 months apart → works

Give the pile time to refill and the sale fires properly. This is the safe cadence.

The takeaway: 2 months is the floor, never tighter. Every quick back-to-back sale flopped (880, then ~11k). Every well-spaced one worked or boomed (18k, 50k, 90k, 100k). "Every 2 months" sits right on the edge — it works, but you cannot compress it further.

Note: Jun & Jul 2024 are excluded — that was the game's launch surge (fresh demand, full pile), so even a back-to-back sale rode the launch wave. Those are a one-time exception, not the steady-state pattern, so we leave them out of the cadence comparison.

Two things we tell BNE straight

We trade global reach for control

Some past spikes rode the global Steam sale's discovery and front-page featuring. A China-only window won't get that push — it leans entirely on the China channel's own reach. Whether the channel replaces that traffic is the real open question. The data can't answer it; we flag it, we don't hide it.

The year becomes fully promo-paced

Six windows a year risks training buyers to only ever wait for a discount. But the data shows they already behave exactly that way — the base game sells nothing at full price regardless. So there's no full-price business to lose. The risk is largely already priced in.

Why this beats waiting for the global calendar: we pick the dates, we wall the discount off from global + Switch 2 pricing permanently, and we convert a pile that's otherwise stranded — on our schedule, six times a year, instead of whenever Steam's global sales happen to land.
