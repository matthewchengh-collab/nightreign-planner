The spacing law — proven by every discount we ran

Each bar is a base-game discount event (launch months Jun–Jul 2024 excluded — see note). The pattern is blunt: run two sales too close and the second one flops.

✗ 1 month apart → flops

A sale right after another barely sells. The pile was just drained — nothing left to convert.

✓ 2–3 months apart → works

Give the pile time to refill and the sale fires properly. This is the safe cadence.

The takeaway: 2 months is the floor, never tighter. Every quick back-to-back sale flopped (880, then ~11k). Every well-spaced one worked or boomed (18k, 50k, 90k, 100k). "Every 2 months" sits right on the edge — it works, but you cannot compress it further.

Note: Jun & Jul 2024 are excluded — that was the game's launch surge (fresh demand, full pile), so even a back-to-back sale rode the launch wave. A one-time exception, not the steady-state pattern.

Two things we tell BNE straight

We trade global reach for control

Some past spikes rode the global Steam sale's discovery and front-page featuring. A China-only window won't get that push — it leans entirely on the China channel's own reach. Whether the channel replaces that traffic is the real open question. We flag it, we don't hide it.

The year becomes fully promo-paced

Several windows a year risks training buyers to only ever wait for a discount. But the data shows they already behave that way — the base game sells nothing at full price regardless. So there's no full-price business to lose. The risk is largely already priced in.

Watch the pile drain

Gold = redeemed by the cadence. Grey = inventory still sitting in the channel after the year.

The thing to watch — October starves December. Base game runs three windows (Oct, Dec, Apr) off one fixed 26k pile, no reorder. October fires first into the full pile, so it pulls the most — which means December, your big month, gets what October left behind. The pile doesn't know December is special; it gives codes to whoever discounts first. If December is the month you want biggest, October base is working against you — either run Oct lighter, or move base off October entirely (it has 85k of DLC pile sitting idle). Drag the base slider and watch December shrink as October grows.

Why this is a floor, not a ceiling: we can't yet model the pile refilling — the channel reorders codes during quiet months (the loading pattern), but we don't have the intake rate. This model assumes a fixed pile that only drains, so real numbers are likely higher. Conservative on purpose.

Solid vs assumed: the 110,711 pile and per-SKU split are straight from the Stock tab. The conversion % per window is the estimate — anchored to how hard past windows drained, firm it up against real intake data later.
