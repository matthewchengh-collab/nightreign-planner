The big picture
China has a major shopping festival in 11 of 12 months — but Elden Ring only ever activated on the 5 that happened to land on a Steam sale. Six China-native events (520, 8.8, 818, 9.9, Double 11, CNY) ran at full price, no promo. We've been running a Steam-only playbook in a market that shops year-round. SEGA & Cyberpunk work these festivals every month; we never have. That's the gap — bigger than "Nov & Feb," it's the whole CN-native calendar.

The finding
ER's full-price strategy beats SEGA's discount-everything strategy almost every month, often 4–9×. November isn't actually weak in revenue (ER ¥2.08M vs SEGA ¥1.21M) — it only looked weak in units because ER sells fewer keys at much higher prices. February is the one genuine soft spot — the single month a discounting competitor out-earned us (SEGA ¥2.25M vs ER ¥1.91M), because CNY + Steam LNY is when China buyers are most discount-hungry. That's the one window where testing a controlled ER promo might be worth it.
